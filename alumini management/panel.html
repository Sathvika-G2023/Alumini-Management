PANNEL DISCUSSION


A panel discussion is a structured event where a group of experts share their perspectives on a topic. The goal is to stimulate critical thinking and provide the audience with new insights.



What happens in a panel discussion?

A moderator or presenter guides the discussion
Panelists share their knowledge, opinions, and experiences
The audience can ask questions
The moderator summarizes the discussion and opens it up to the audience

What types of panel discussions are there?

In-person: The panelists and audience are in the same room
Virtual: The panelists and audience are connected online
Hybrid: The panelists and audience are both in-person and online

When are panel discussions used?

When are panel discussions used?
In teaching, where students can act as panelists and the rest of the class acts as the audience

How can you participate in a panel discussion?

Research the topic and other panelists
Come up with examples and stories to illustrate your points
Be prepared to answer questions from the moderator or audience
